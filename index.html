I explore how individuals navigate economic life through the lens of high-frequency transaction data. As a PhD researcher at the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration of Ghent University under supervision of Kris Boudt and Koen Schoors, I combine physics-inspired methodology with machine learning and econometrics to study how households respond to income changes.

My research harnesses the power of individual-level administrative data to understand the marginal propensity to consume, aiming to shed new light on household finance and inequality. At BNP Paribas Fortis, I'm transforming banking data into actionable insights that bridge the gap between academic research and real-world impact. Throughout this work, I'm committed to the principles of open science and reproducible research.

Beyond financial data, I develop side projects at the intersection of data science and endurance sports, experimenting with visualization techniques and routing algorithms for cycling analytics. I'm always eager to collaborate on initiatives that combine technical innovation with societal impact.
